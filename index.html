About me

I'm Allison, an investigative reporter and data journalist.

I spent the last few years covering the state's largest school district at the Courier-Journal in Louisville, Kentucky, and I'm now moving onto the company's investigative team. I formerly worked at The Palm Beach Post and at Bankrate.

In addition to being a journo and data nerd, I'm an amateur baker just trying to make a successful sourdough bread.

Recent work

After hearing several anecdotes about students in schools being held down or confined to a room, I started pulling public records to understand the scope of the use of the practices. The first major finding: the state's largest school district had significantly underreported the number of times restraint and seclusion were being used - and it knew it had done so.

I have been focused on reporting on disparities in student discipline in schools. Mining district data sets has yielded stories never been told before, including about how schools don't always follow the district's code of conduct when doling out punishments - sometimes handing out suspensions for as simple a thing as being tardy.
